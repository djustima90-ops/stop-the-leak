1 Lead Leaks

No visible phone number anywhere on the site High
Impact: High-intent enterprise prospects who prefer speaking with sales immediately have no direct contact option, forcing them through form friction.
Fix: Add a clickable phone number in the header and footer with text like "Call Sales: [number]" or "Speak to an Expert: [number]".
No clickable tel: links present High
Impact: Mobile users cannot tap-to-call, creating additional friction on mobile devices where phone calls are the easiest conversion action.
Fix: Implement tel: links alongside displayed phone numbers once added.
No visible email address for direct contact Medium
Impact: Prospects who prefer email communication or have technical questions must go through form submission without alternative contact methods.
Fix: Add a general sales or info email address in footer and contact sections (e.g., "sales@bynder.com" or "contact@bynder.com").
No live chat widget detected Medium
Impact: Real-time questions from engaged visitors cannot be answered immediately, potentially losing warm leads during their research phase.
Fix: Implement a live chat widget for instant engagement, especially during business hours for enterprise prospects.
No Google Business/Maps integration visible Low
Impact: Local search visibility and trust signals from Google presence are missing, though less critical for enterprise SaaS.
Fix: Embed Google Maps showing office locations if applicable, or link to Google Business profiles.

2 Conversion Leaks

Multiple competing CTAs above the fold ("Schedule a demo," "Discovery call with an expert," "Get pricing info") Medium
Impact: Decision paralysis from three different primary CTAs may reduce conversion rate by splitting visitor attention.
Fix: Consolidate to one primary CTA above the fold (likely "Schedule a demo") with secondary options below or in navigation.
Form requires US state selection but shows dropdown before country is selected Low
Impact: Form friction for international visitors who must navigate irrelevant US state field, potentially causing form abandonment.
Fix: Make state field conditional, only appearing after "United States" is selected in country dropdown.
Form has 17 inputs including multiple hidden fields Low
Impact: High field count can increase form abandonment, though hidden fields don't directly affect user experience.
Fix: Audit form fields and reduce visible required fields to absolute minimum (name, email, company maximum).
